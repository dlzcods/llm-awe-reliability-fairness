📖 Metric Glossary
ICC (Intraclass Correlation Coefficient)
Measures reliability/consistency between ratings. Range 0-1. ICC > 0.75 = excellent reliability, 0.60-0.75 = good, < 0.60 = moderate/poor.
Mean Total Score
Average essay score across all rubric dimensions (grammar, lexical, coherence, task). Higher = better scoring.
Auto-Approve
Percentage of essays that can be auto-scored without human review. Based on low CV (< 0.15) and small range (≤ 2).
CV (Coefficient of Variation)
Standard deviation / mean. Measures score variance relative to the mean. Lower = more consistent.
Range
Max score - min score across model runs for the same essay. Lower = more consistent scoring.
t-test (Severity Bias)
Statistical test comparing mean scores between models. p < 0.05 indicates significant difference.
HITL (Human-in-the-Loop)
Essays flagged for manual review due to high variance or inconsistency across runs.
Topic Bias (Delta)
How much a model's score deviates from its average for a specific topic. Positive = scores above average.
